About Me

I am a PhD student in the Medical Engineering and Medical Physics program at the Harvard-MIT Program in Health Sciences & Technology and a member of the Shalek Lab at MIT, the Ragon Institute, and the Broad Institute. Previously, I graduated summa cum laude and with departmental distinction in biomedical engineering from Duke University in 2018. There, I conducted my undergraduate research under John Reif and Ashutosh Chilkoti.

My research interests lie at the intersection of biomolecular technology development and global health - engineering single-cell profiling techniques to probe the ensemble behavior of biological systems in the context of infectious disease.

Outside of the lab, I enjoy sailing, scuba diving, hiking, and cheering on Duke Basketball.
