About Me

I am a first year PhD student in the Medical Engineering and Medical Physics program at the Harvard-MIT Program in Health Sciences & Technology working in the Shalek Lab. Previously, I graduated summa cum laude and with departmental distinction in biomedical engineering from Duke University. There, I conducted my undergraduate research under John Reif and Ashutosh Chilkoti.

My research interests lie at the intersection of biomolecular technology development and global health - designing single-cell profiling techniques for probing the ensemble behavior of cells in the context of infectious disease.

Outside of the lab, I enjoy sailing, scuba diving, hiking, and cheering on Duke Basketball.
